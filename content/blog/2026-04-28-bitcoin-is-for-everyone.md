There's no shortage of Bitcoin events on the calendar these days --- online summits, niche meetups, high-priced conferences in flashy venues. But if you're in the Pacific Northwest and you're serious about Bitcoin, there's one gathering that stands in a class of its own: Bitcoin Is for Everyone, returning for its third year on May 22–23, 2026 in Portland, Oregon.

This isn't a hype fest. It isn't a trading seminar. It's something rarer: a genuinely welcoming, education-first Bitcoin conference designed for everyone --- whether you've never bought your first sat or you've been stacking since 2013.

## What Makes Bitcoin Is for Everyone Different?

Walk into most crypto events and you'll find slick marketing, inflated promises, and a lot of noise. Bitcoin Is for Everyone takes a different approach. The tagline says it all: *Two Days. One Movement. Zero Hype.*

The conference is built around three core ideas:

**Build Your Conviction.** Talks and workshops are designed to cut through the noise. There's no fluff and no altcoin shilling --- just honest, deep dives into why Bitcoin matters and how it works. Whether you're brand new to the idea of sound money or you've already done the reading, you'll leave with sharper thinking and stronger conviction.

**Find Your People.** There's a reason "Real community. Not Twitter." is one of the event's guiding principles. This is a place to have real conversations --- the kind that actually go somewhere. Two days surrounded by curious, thoughtful people who share a genuine interest in Bitcoin's potential can change your perspective in ways that no tweet thread ever will.

**Leave Ready.** This isn't inspiration porn. The goal isn't to send you home fired up with nothing to show for it. Bitcoin Is for Everyone is structured so you walk away with tools, resources, and real human connections --- a corner of support for whatever your next steps with Bitcoin look like.

## The Venues

The conference spreads across two of Portland's most beloved spaces, which perfectly mirrors its character: rooted in community, accessible, and distinctly Pacific Northwest.

**Friday, May 22 — Workshops at the Kennedy School**

McMenamins' Kennedy School is a converted 1915 elementary school turned community gathering space, complete with soaking pool, movie theater, and rooms full of character. It's the ideal setting for intimate, hands-on workshops where you can actually dig in, ask questions, and learn alongside fellow attendees.

**Saturday, May 23 — Main Conference at Revolution Hall**

Revolution Hall, a beautifully restored 1924 high school in Portland's inner east side, hosts the main conference day. It's a storied venue that has welcomed some of the most important voices in music and culture --- and now Bitcoin. Expect keynotes, panel discussions, and the kind of high-energy programming that makes you wish the day wouldn't end.

## Side Events: Before, During, and After

The conference experience extends beyond the main programming. There's a Pre-Conference Party to kick things off and a Post-Conference Dinner to close it out --- because some of the best conversations happen away from the stage. These side events are a chance to connect more deeply with speakers and fellow attendees in a relaxed setting.

## This Is the Premier Bitcoin Event in the Pacific Northwest

Oregon and the broader Pacific Northwest have a quietly thriving Bitcoin community --- curious, independent-minded, and deeply values-driven. Bitcoin Is for Everyone is the community's annual gathering point, and with each year the event has grown in depth, reach, and reputation.

Now in its third year, the conference has earned its place as the region's flagship Bitcoin education event. If you want to understand what Bitcoin means for your financial future, for your community, and for the world --- this is where that conversation is happening.
